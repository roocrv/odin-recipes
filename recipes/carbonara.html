Carbonara

Image by mrsiraphol on Freepik

Description

Carbonara is a classical italian dish made with guanciale, eggs, cheese (usually Pecorino Romano), spaghetti, and black pepper. Some countries have adapted their recipes to include cream, milk, garlic, onions, and other variations but this is not so common amongst Italians. The authentic Carbonara is simple, but truly delicious.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil
  • Guanciale
  • Spaghetti
  • Eggs
  • Cheese
  • Seasonings

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the guanciale and cook, turning occasionally, until evenly browned and crispy (usually five to ten minutes).
  2. Once cooked, remove from heat and drain the excess oil on paper towels.
  3. Cook your spaghetti in boiling water for about eight minutes. Drain and return to the pot.
  4. Whisk eggs in a bowl. Add in the cheese and black pepper, and whisk again until creamy.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, stirring quickly, until creamy.
  6. Add the guanciale to the pasta, and mix evenly.
  7. Top with the remaining cheese, add more black pepper and serve.
  8. Buon appetito!
